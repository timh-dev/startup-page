# OpenWeather Conditions Visual Guide

This document is a visual interpretation guide for the standard [OpenWeather weather conditions documentation](https://openweathermap.org/weather-conditions?utm_source=chatgpt.com) and how each condition can map to imagery, backgrounds, animations, or AI prompts. The goal is to translate the meteorological meaning into a cinematic visual scene.

OpenWeather conditions are grouped into:

1. Thunderstorm (2xx)
2. Drizzle (3xx)
3. Rain (5xx)
4. Snow (6xx)
5. Atmosphere (7xx)
6. Clear (800)
7. Clouds (80x)

## Group 2xx: Thunderstorm

These almost always imply strong vertical cloud development, typically cumulonimbus clouds.

### 200: Thunderstorm with light rain

Visual:
Dark towering cumulonimbus clouds with scattered rain streaks. Occasional lightning flashes inside distant cloud masses. Wet pavement reflections. Atmosphere feels unstable but not fully severe.

Cloud types:
Cumulonimbus calvus transitioning into mature cumulonimbus.

Details:
- Low contrast daylight
- Soft rolling thunder glow
- Patchy rain curtains
- Blue gray sky gradients

### 201: Thunderstorm with rain

Visual:
Dense storm clouds with consistent rainfall and visible lightning forks cutting through the sky. Rain visibly distorts distant scenery.

Cloud types:
Mature cumulonimbus.

Details:
- Frequent lightning
- Heavy cloud shelf structures
- Dark wet roads
- Mist rising from surfaces

### 202: Thunderstorm with heavy rain

Visual:
Violent storm system with black cumulonimbus towers, torrential rain sheets, rapid lightning bursts, and near white flashes illuminating the entire sky.

Cloud types:
Large mature cumulonimbus incus (anvil top).

Details:
- Near horizontal rain from wind
- Cloud to ground lightning
- Localized flooding
- Dense rain haze
- Very low visibility

### 210: Light thunderstorm

Visual:
Tall isolated storm clouds with intermittent lightning and little precipitation. Dramatic sky but lighter surface effects.

Cloud types:
Developing cumulonimbus.

Details:
- Warm humid air
- Visible cloud growth
- Dark isolated storm cell

### 211: Thunderstorm

Visual:
Classic thunderstorm scene with large cumulonimbus towers dominating the sky. Repeated lightning, heavy shadows, and dynamic cloud movement.

Cloud types:
Cumulonimbus incus.

Details:
- Sharp cloud contrast
- Electric atmosphere
- Rolling thunder ambiance

### 212: Heavy thunderstorm

Visual:
Extremely dark storm front with aggressive lightning activity, thick rain shafts, rotating cloud bases, and violent weather energy.

Cloud types:
Severe cumulonimbus supercell structures possible.

Details:
- Greenish sky tint possible
- Massive cloud anvils
- Intense precipitation
- High wind motion

### 221: Ragged thunderstorm

Visual:
Chaotic broken storm cells with uneven cloud formations and scattered lightning in multiple directions.

Cloud types:
Fragmented cumulonimbus clusters.

Details:
- Turbulent atmosphere
- Irregular rain bands
- Multiple storm layers

### 230 to 232: Thunderstorm with drizzle

Visual:
Low visibility storm with fine misty rain illuminated by lightning flashes. Softer precipitation texture than standard thunderstorms.

Cloud types:
Embedded cumulonimbus inside nimbostratus layers.

Details:
- Foggy storm atmosphere
- Moody blue gray palette
- Wet haze everywhere

## Group 3xx: Drizzle

Drizzle usually comes from low stratus or nimbostratus clouds rather than towering storms.

### 300: Light intensity drizzle

Visual:
Soft gray overcast sky with extremely fine rain particles barely visible in the air.

Cloud types:
Stratus.

Details:
- Muted lighting
- Moist sidewalks
- Gentle atmospheric haze

### 301: Drizzle

Visual:
Steady fine mist rain with low hanging clouds and diffuse lighting.

Cloud types:
Stratus or nimbostratus.

Details:
- Cloud ceiling feels low
- No sharp shadows
- Persistent dampness

### 302: Heavy intensity drizzle

Visual:
Dense mist rain reducing visibility. Surfaces appear soaked despite the absence of large raindrops.

Cloud types:
Thick nimbostratus.

Details:
- Dense atmospheric softness
- Cool gray tones
- Constant wet sheen

### 310 to 312: Drizzle rain combinations

Visual:
Transition zone between drizzle and proper rainfall. Thin rain streaks beneath solid overcast skies.

Cloud types:
Nimbostratus.

Details:
- Soft but visible rainfall
- Windshield blur effect
- Urban reflections

### 313 and 314: Shower rain and drizzle

Visual:
Intermittent rain bursts mixed with lingering mist beneath unstable layered clouds.

Cloud types:
Broken nimbostratus with convective pockets.

Details:
- Variable visibility
- Uneven rain intensity
- Dark patches in sky

### 321: Shower drizzle

Visual:
Localized drifting drizzle showers with patches of brighter sky between clouds.

Cloud types:
Broken stratus.

Details:
- Passing wet bands
- Light wind
- Silver cloud edges

## Group 5xx: Rain

Rain conditions generally imply nimbostratus or deep layered cloud systems.

### 500: Light rain

Visual:
Gentle steady rainfall under medium gray skies. Individual raindrops visible.

Cloud types:
Nimbostratus.

Details:
- Soft reflections
- Wet vegetation
- Mild atmospheric blur

### 501: Moderate rain

Visual:
Consistent rainfall with darker cloud cover and visible rain streak density.

Cloud types:
Dense nimbostratus.

Details:
- Street puddles
- Moderate visibility loss
- Muted colors

### 502: Heavy intensity rain

Visual:
Strong rainfall creating splashes, runoff streams, and reduced visibility.

Cloud types:
Deep nimbostratus with embedded convection.

Details:
- Fast moving rain
- Strong surface reflections
- Wind ripples in puddles

### 503: Very heavy rain

Visual:
Near tropical downpour with thick rain curtains and highly obscured distance.

Details:
- Rain dominates scene
- Gray washed atmosphere
- Heavy runoff

### 504: Extreme rain

Visual:
Violent rainfall with near white rain density, flooding streets, and barely visible skyline.

Details:
- Storm drainage overflow
- Continuous water motion
- Severe weather mood

### 511: Freezing rain

Visual:
Cold gray atmosphere with icy rain coating surfaces in reflective glaze.

Cloud types:
Nimbostratus in freezing conditions.

Details:
- Ice accumulation
- Glossy frozen roads
- Cold blue tones

### 520 to 531: Shower rain variants

Visual:
Convective rain bursts beneath broken clouds rather than solid overcast.

Cloud types:
Cumulus congestus or broken cumulonimbus.

Details:
- Localized rain shafts
- Sunlight peeking through gaps
- Dynamic sky contrast

For 531 "ragged shower rain":
Chaotic uneven rain cells with fragmented cloud formations.

## Group 6xx: Snow

### 600: Light snow

Visual:
Sparse drifting snowflakes beneath pale overcast skies.

Cloud types:
Stratus or altostratus.

Details:
- Quiet atmosphere
- Soft lighting
- Gentle accumulation

### 601: Snow

Visual:
Steady snowfall with thick cloud cover and accumulating snow layers.

Cloud types:
Nimbostratus in cold conditions.

Details:
- Muted soundscape
- Low contrast environment
- Snow coated trees

### 602: Heavy snow

Visual:
Dense snowfall dramatically reducing visibility.

Details:
- Large flakes
- Whiteout effect beginning
- Snow blowing sideways

### 611: Sleet

Visual:
Mix of snow and icy rain creating wet slushy surfaces.

Details:
- Partially melted snow
- Reflective roads
- Cold wet atmosphere

### 612 to 613: Shower sleet

Visual:
Intermittent icy precipitation bands with unstable winter clouds.

Details:
- Mixed precipitation textures
- Wind gust motion

### 615 to 616: Rain and snow mix

Visual:
Hybrid precipitation with slush, melting snowflakes, and wet roads.

Cloud types:
Nimbostratus.

Details:
- Gray winter palette
- Heavy moisture content

### 620 to 622: Shower snow

Visual:
Localized snow bursts beneath broken winter clouds.

Cloud types:
Convective snow clouds.

Details:
- Patchy visibility
- Variable snowfall density

## Group 7xx: Atmosphere

These are the most visually interpretive conditions.

### 701: Mist

Visual:
Thin low level moisture reducing distant clarity slightly.

Details:
- Soft atmospheric diffusion
- Faded horizon
- Morning humidity feel

### 711: Smoke

Visual:
Dry smoky haze with warm desaturated lighting.

Details:
- Orange sunlight diffusion
- Reduced visibility
- Air quality distortion

### 721: Haze

Visual:
Bright washed out atmosphere with softened distant objects.

Details:
- Pale sky
- Low contrast skyline
- Sun glow bloom

### 731 and 761: Dust / dust storms

Visual:
Airborne tan dust clouds moving across dry landscapes.

Details:
- Desert atmosphere
- Reduced visibility
- Warm earth tones

### 741: Fog

Visual:
Dense ground level cloud obscuring distance almost completely.

Cloud types:
Ground touching stratus.

Details:
- Diffused lights
- Strong atmospheric depth
- Soft silhouettes

### 751: Sand

Visual:
Windblown sand particles creating a desert haze.

Details:
- Golden brown sky
- Harsh sunlight diffusion

### 762: Volcanic ash

Visual:
Dark ash filled atmosphere with heavy suspended particles.

Details:
- Apocalyptic lighting
- Gray black skies
- Ash drifting in air

### 771: Squalls

Visual:
Sudden violent wind bursts with rapidly moving low clouds and blowing debris.

Cloud types:
Fast moving stratocumulus or cumulonimbus fragments.

Details:
- Bent trees
- Horizontal precipitation
- Dynamic motion

### 781: Tornado

Visual:
Massive rotating funnel beneath severe supercell thunderstorm clouds.

Cloud types:
Supercell cumulonimbus.

Details:
- Green black sky
- Debris rotation
- Extreme atmospheric tension

## Group 800: Clear

### 800: Clear sky

Visual:
Clean open sky with strong sunlight and sharp visibility.

Cloud types:
None.

Details:
- Deep blue sky gradient
- Crisp shadows
- Bright atmosphere

Night:
- Clear star visibility
- Moon glow
- Deep navy sky

## Group 80x: Clouds

These benefit the most from cloud type interpretation.

### 801: Few clouds

Visual:
Mostly clear sky with isolated small cumulus clouds.

Cloud types:
Fair weather cumulus.

Details:
- Bright sunlight
- Soft white cloud puffs
- High visibility

### 802: Scattered clouds

Visual:
Broken distribution of fluffy cumulus clouds across a blue sky.

Cloud types:
Cumulus mediocris.

Details:
- Alternating sun and shade
- Dynamic sky texture

### 803: Broken clouds

Visual:
Large fragmented cloud coverage with visible sky gaps.

Cloud types:
Stratocumulus.

Details:
- Layered cloud decks
- Sun beams through openings
- Partial overcast feel

This is where "broken clouds" becomes visually interesting:
Think textured stratocumulus sheets with irregular tearing patterns and soft depth.

### 804: Overcast clouds

Visual:
Complete cloud blanket covering the sky uniformly.

Cloud types:
Stratus or thick stratocumulus.

Details:
- Flat diffuse lighting
- No direct shadows
- Cool gray tones

If darker:
Transition toward nimbostratus.

## Best Mapping Philosophy

A strong weather visual system should combine:

Condition + cloud structure + lighting + visibility + surface interaction + motion.

For example:

"Light rain" should not just mean "raindrops." It should imply nimbostratus clouds, wet reflections, softened contrast, muted lighting, and distant atmospheric blur.

"Broken clouds" should not just mean random clouds. It should imply fragmented stratocumulus layers, alternating sunlight patches, visible sky gaps, and dynamic shadow movement.

Primary source for condition taxonomy: [OpenWeather Weather Conditions](https://openweathermap.org/weather-conditions?utm_source=chatgpt.com).
